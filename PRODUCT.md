# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Mixed audience sharing one tool: marketing managers and media buyers reviewing lead sources and campaign performance; sales reps and team leads tracking pipeline and appointments; and a founder or exec checking full-funnel health. All three read the same data but ask different questions. The marketing person wants to know where leads come from and which sources convert. The sales lead wants to know where deals are stuck. The exec wants to know if the business is healthy at a glance.

## Product Purpose

An internal operations dashboard that surfaces GoHighLevel CRM data in two views (Marketing and Sales). Its job is to let the team answer the question "what's actually working?" without exporting to a spreadsheet. Success means a user can open the dashboard, spot a pattern or problem, and take action in under two minutes.

## Brand Personality

Serious, confident, precise. Stripe Dashboard as the reference point: clean data hierarchy, every element earns its place, nothing decorative, no apologies. The interface communicates that the people using it are professionals who respect their own time.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS blue-and-white dashboard templates: looks like a starter kit, not a tool anyone trusts with business decisions.
- Heavy BI tool aesthetics (Tableau, Cognos): overwhelming chrome, filter panels everywhere, too many controls visible at once. Operational dashboards should feel decisive, not exploratory.

## Design Principles

1. **Data earns the surface.** Every visual element must carry information or help the user understand it faster. No ambient gradients, no decorative shapes, no patterns that exist for aesthetic reasons alone.
2. **Hierarchy over uniformity.** The most important number on any screen should feel twice as prominent as the second. A flat visual scale is a failure of prioritization.
3. **Confident presentation.** Numbers are shown with conviction. Empty states and loading states are purposeful, not apologetic. Hedging language ("possibly", "approximately") is never used in labels.
4. **Speed as respect.** The user is mid-decision. Interactions are immediate. Transitions exist only when they carry meaning, and they are brief.
5. **Consistent precision.** Numbers are always formatted with the same logic throughout a view. No rounding that could mislead across charts that share the same dataset.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Internal tool; no formal WCAG requirement. Standard contrast ratios and keyboard navigation are sufficient. No known user needs for reduced motion or color blindness accommodations, but charts should never use color as the sole data encoding (labels, tooltips, and patterns supplement color).
